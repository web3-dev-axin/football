# PolyGoal Market Homepage Design

Date: 2026-05-26

## Goal

Create a complete single-page design for PolyGoal, a World Cup 2026 prediction market. The page should reference Polymarket's dense market-browsing experience while preserving PolyGoal's football-specific focus and green brand theme.

## Direction

Use a market-first homepage rather than a generic sports schedule. The first screen should communicate:

- PolyGoal is a live prediction market, not only a fixture list.
- Users can trade match winner and exact score outcomes.
- The product supports wallet connection on X Layer.
- The design feels credible, liquid, and fast.

## Page Structure

1. Top navigation with brand, market categories, portfolio, settlements, search, and wallet action.
2. Hero market board with live match state, probability cards, liquidity, volume, and featured trade actions.
3. Date and category filters for rapid fixture browsing.
4. Featured markets grid combining live and upcoming matches.
5. Side insight rail with wallet balance, trending teams, market momentum, and settlement status.
6. Bottom section for upcoming high-volume markets and exact-score opportunities.

## Visual System

- Primary theme: fresh green on white, grounded by dark ink and quiet gray surfaces.
- Accent colors: red only for live status and "No" outcome; yellow only for attention/odds movement.
- Typography: strong grotesque headlines, compact table/card text, numeric emphasis for prices.
- Components: dense cards, segmented controls, compact buttons, small status pills, probability bars.
- Mood: polished trading desk meets football broadcast, without becoming a sportsbook landing page.

## Interaction Scope

The static prototype should include lightweight interactions:

- Date/category chip active states.
- Market card hover states.
- Yes/No button selection feedback.
- Watchlist toggles.
- Search filtering over visible market cards.

## Implementation

Use plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. No build step is required. The page should open directly in a browser or be served by a simple local static server for verification.

## Verification

Check desktop and mobile screenshots. Confirm no major text overlap, cards remain stable, and the page has a complete first-screen market experience.
